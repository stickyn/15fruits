Tale 2:

Chloe’s eyes were infatuated by the light her word document seemed to spew. It was a perfect setup, a computer setup right beside a big window, that projected a gray dark night with an angry but relaxing thunderstorm. Her eyes pointed towards the clock, ignited by the light provided by the occasional lighting strikes. 11:24 PM. Buzzz. Her eyes slowly slid to the left of her monitor, a small landline with a tiny green display, barely visible even when slightly illuminated, showcased the words: “NIGHTE” Chloe slowly picked up the phone. “How are you?” A voice that enters Chloe’s ears said. “Never better.” Chloe responded. “Do you enjoy the current weather?” The voice asked. “Of course. You feeling tired?” Chloe asked. “I don’t really do that.” Chloe didn’t know what to think about that. “What’s your name?” Chloe finally asked. “What would you like it to be?” “What does ‘NIGHTE’ mean?” “I don’t have control over that. Whatever the...soul makes up.” “Hmm, is there something you wanted to talk about?” A strange pause occurred. Chloe and the voice over the phone both went silent, not a sound, Chloe didn’t breathe. Nor did the voice. “I guess I will talk to you tomorrow.” The voice finally said. “I look forward to it,” Chloe responded before putting the phone back, turning off her computer and slowly becoming apart of the black void that surrounded the office
